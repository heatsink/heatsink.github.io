Autonomous Golf Cart            >

Autonomous Golf Cart

TL;DR: collision detection & navigation, ROS, and home-made PCBs. Even more ROS, Python, lidar, and other lesser sensors (just kidding, they're all essential).

The autonomous golf cart is one of three flagship projects that the Cal Poly Robotics team is currently dedicated to. I'm part of what we affectionally call the IGVC team, even though we don't (yet) compete in the Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition. But we do challenge and compete against ourselves to do some quality engineering! Or something like that...

Within the IGVC team, I aim to primarily focus on collision detection & navigation. We use a SICK 2D Lidar, which coincidentally allows for many great puns and references as with the secondary benefit of allowing me to write collision detection software. We are currently experimenting with ROS's navigation stack for inspiration and proof of concept. I will begin designing our own in-house navigation stack during Fall 2016.

What's a good large-scale engineering project without a solid definition for success? The project can be declared successful when the golf cart can drive to and from the nearest Woodstock's Pizza and return with a dozen large Pepperonies. And maybe some Man Cave Wings.

Even though I focus on software within the Robotics team, I still interact with hardware on a regular basis. This PCB was designed by my current team members before I joined the club, and I will design a module to add onto the board for a speedcontroller during Spring 2016.
